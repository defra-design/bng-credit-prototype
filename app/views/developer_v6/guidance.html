Record off-site gain before you discharge your BNG planning condition

By law, you need to record any off-site land you're using to make up at least 10% biodiversity net gain for your development project. Your local planning authority sets the exact percentage of net gain required.

If the information you provide is accepted, you'll need to add the details to your Gain Plan and submit them to your local planning authority.

Before you start

You'll need:

  • the Biodiversity Metric file which your local planning authority approved for your Biodiversity Net Gain Statement
  • the biodiversity gain site number for the off-site land
  • to know the number of biodiversity units you're using for each habitat type
  • to have consent from the landowner for any gain site you're using
