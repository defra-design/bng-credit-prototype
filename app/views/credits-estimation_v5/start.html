Estimate the cost of biodiversity statutory credits

Use this service to estimate the cost of biodiversity gain credits on a development.

If your development is unable to achieve minimum 10% biodiversity net gain (BNG) through on-site and off-site habitat, you can purchase a statutory biodiversity credit.

What is a statutory biodiversity credit?

The statutory biodiversity credit scheme is a last resort.

A statutory biodiversity credit can only be bought from the Secretary of State at a price set by Defra. The Proof of Purchase should then be included in a developer’s Biodiversity Net Gain Plan, to demonstrate that credits have been purchased.

Eligibility

To be eligible, a development must provide evidence to the Local Planning Authority (LPA) that on-site and off-site options are not available.

Credit type

We need to know which credit type you intend to use for your biodiversity gain credits.

Your credit type will either be Area, Hedgerow or Watercourse. Area measurements are categorised in credit tiers from A1 to A5.

Hedgerow's credit tier is H. Watercourse's credit tier is W.

The revenue from the credits will then be invested in habitat delivery in England.

The digital sales service is currently being built and guidance will be published before BNG becomes mandatory across England.
