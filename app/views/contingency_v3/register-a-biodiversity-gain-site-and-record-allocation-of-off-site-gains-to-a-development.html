Register a biodiversity gain site and record allocation of off-site gains to a development

You can apply to register a biodiversity gain site, and record allocation of off-site gains to a development, if you’re:

  • a landowner or leaseholder
  • an agent acting on behalf of a landowner or leaseholder as an individual
  • an agent acting on behalf of a landowner or leaseholder as an organisation

Before you apply

If you're an agent, the landowner or leaseholder of the gain site you're registering must give consent. They will need to complete the 'Authorise an agent to act on your behalf' form. This must be sent with your application.

How to apply

  1. You will need to download and complete the relevant forms to register a biodiversity gain site and record allocation of off-site gains to a development. These will need to be sent to Natural England to be processed.
  2. When we email you back, we will ask you to pay the £639 registration and allocation fee. We will also ask you to send:
    • a planning obligation (section 106) or a conservation covenant that secures the site for at least 30 years
    • a map showing the land boundary
    • the title deeds or lease agreement to prove ownership
    • a local land charge certificate
    • a completed Biodiversity Metric 4.0
    • a habitat management and monitoring plan (this may be within your legal agreement)

    Find more information on documents you will need to send us.

  3. We will carry out verification checks against your documents.
  4. We will let you know if you have been accepted, rejected or if we need more information from you.

Withdrawals and refunds

Once we have made a decision on your application you cannot withdraw it or apply for a refund.

You may be able to get a refund if we have not made a decision within 6 weeks. Find out more information in the terms and conditions.
